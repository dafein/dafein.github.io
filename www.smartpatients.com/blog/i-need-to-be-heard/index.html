I am the patient and I need to be heard

A beautiful thing happened yesterday. A young woman posted a short video from her hospital room:

We hear her saying: respect me. There’s a lot to say about how doctors, patients, and caregivers communicate with each other. Here we just want to share a story about the impact this video has already had. Within hours of it being posted, a physician in Maine named Peter Elias saw the video and showed it to a teenage boy in his office who was recently hospitalized and felt more like a piece of furniture than a patient.

The boy was quiet for a bit and then said, “She get’s it.” The next time he’s admitted, he plans to have this video queued up on his iPad. “If they want to examine me or draw blood, they’re gonna have to watch this first.”

The way in which we’re connected today means this young woman has just empowered this young man in a way that perhaps no one else could have. Thank you, Peter, for helping make this happen, for sharing this story with us, and for getting the family’s permission for us to comment on it here.
