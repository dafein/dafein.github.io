Sarcoma research in the last 20 years

November 22, 2014Roni Zeiger

Have a look at the graph above representing the evolution of sarcoma trials during the last 20 years. 1995 was significant. We were building the first ACOR online communities and the rapid growth in the number of clinical trials accepting sarcoma patients had begun. However, as you can see, relatively few of the phase 1 and phase 2 trials were followed by phase 3 trials, which are the ones that lead to approval of new drugs.

Cancer patients suffering from most solid tumors today have many more treatment options than they had 15 years ago, thanks to new targeted therapies and a growing number of trials for various immunotherapies. Unfortunately, that is not the reality for patients with many types of sarcoma. While we see significant activity testing anti-PD-1 and anti-PD-L1 therapies for many cancers, there are few such trials for sarcoma. A more promising observation is the increase in trials for targeted therapies which are accepting sarcoma patients (light blue bars above).

In 1993, a wealthy sarcoma patient invited a group of physicians and sarcomas thought leaders to a meeting, contending that these experts were clinical and scientific experts, but were terrible at communicating with each other. Hence was born an international, multidisciplinary, and ongoing sarcoma dialog known as CTOS (the Connective Tissue Oncology Society). This was an inflection point in the history of sarcoma research. CTOS has cultivated cooperation across many sarcoma advocacy organizations

While sarcoma research overall has grown significantly, funding and advances in the treatment of the many less common sarcomas remains low. The graph below shows how the outcome of this research shrinks as we slice the pie to include more rare subtypes of sarcoma, which still affect many people.

 

We hope that the near future brings more progress to patients and families of this important group of rare cancers. For those who might want to participate in a sarcoma trial, we’ve put together this resource of open sarcoma trials.
