Real cancer stories

September 27, 2013stories and scienceRoni Zeiger

It’s fitting that our first blog post is about stories.  We’ve been talking for years about how much we need stories to provide context to all this big data we’re producing.  They’re also what makes us human, reminds us of what’s important. The stories in our community, from so many generous people who want others to learn from their experience, have made us wonder how we can help these stories soar even further.

It came into focus when we saw this gripping story about disease, life, pain, and love told on stage at TEDMED 2013, and met The Story Collider team, an awesome non-profit that teaches people to share their stories about science on camera.  Before we knew it, our teams (Smart Patients and The Story Collider) had agreed to help share with the world 100 real, raw, inspiring, sometimes heart-breaking stories from cancer patients and caregivers.  Not just about disease, but about living with disease.  Not just about dying, but about living the end of one’s life purposefully.  These stories are all people learning to live and die, with respect, even though they have this ruthless disease.

Perhaps the most important thing about real cancer stories is that they can provide immediate, tangible help to newly diagnosed cancer patients who suddenly feel so lost and alone.

We asked Bonnie, one of the members of our ovarian cancer community, if she would be one of our first subjects.  She has been sharing with the community her journey living with ovarian cancer, and more recently her decision process to choose hospice.  Bonnie loved the idea of this project and wants more people to learn from her experience.  A few days later when I had the privilege of visiting with her in her home, I expected to talk about cancer and dying.  We mostly talked about tango.

That’s Bonnie showing me how to tango.  She got out of bed to show off her red tango shoes.  Her words, her generous spirit, her stories are a gift.  We are so grateful that hers will be the first video of the Real Cancer Stories project.  We’re editing her video now and will share a piece of the video in the next couple of days.  In the meantime, please check out the project: www.smartpatients.com/stories.  You’ll recognize the artwork that’s based on the image above.  I’m sure it will be the cover of the DVD and the poster.
